Timeline of Justin Timberlake's Life

  • 1981 - Born in Memphis, Tennessee.
  • 1993 - Became a Mouseketeer on the all-new Mickey Mouse Club. He worked with future stars like singer Britney Spears, singer Christina Aguilera, singer JC Chasez, actor Ryan Gosling, and actor Keri Russell.
  • 1995 - The boy band NSYNC was formed where Justin was one of the lead singers. They release legendary hits such as "Tearin' Up My Hear", "It's Gonna Be Me", and "Bye Bye Bye". They sold more than 70 million records, making them one of the top boy bands in history.
  • 2002 - NSYNC was disbanded, and Justin went off to become a solo artist. He debuted his first hit single "Like I love You". Justin also was the first celebrity to appear on Ashton Kutcher's comedy show Punk'd.
  • 2003 - First appearance on NBC's Saturday Night Live (SNL). First time meeting comedian Jimmy Fallon. This marked the beginning of a long onging friendship between Justin and Jimmy.
  • 2004 - Paired with Janet Jackson to perform at the halftime show during Super Bowl XXXVIII. There was a wardrobe malfunction that forced Justin and Janet to apologize at the 2004 Grammy Awards in order to accept their awards.
  • 2006 - Release hit album FutureSex/LoveSounds that became the biggest album for pre-orders on iTunes. Justin also appeared on SNL for a second time, performing the viral skit with the song D*** in a Box. This song became one of the most viewed videos on YouTube, and earned Justin an Emmy Award.
  • 2010 - Put a hold on music to pursue more acting roles. Some of the well-known movies he played in are The Social Network, Bad Teacher, and Friends with Benefits.
  • 2012 - Married actress Jessica Biel.
  • 2013 - Justin annouced his return to the music industry. His next biggest album The 20/20 Experience - 2 of 2 was released.
  • 2015 - His firstborn son, Silas Randall Timberlake, was born.
  • 2016 - Voiced the lead character in the musical comedy animated film Trolls alongside Anna Kendrick. The film included the lead single Can't Stop the Feeling!.
  • 2018 - Released his fifth studio album Man of the Woods. Two days later, he headlined the Super Bowl LII halftime show in Minneapolis, Minnesota.
